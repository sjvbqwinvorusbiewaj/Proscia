Lanthanum (La)

Property Value
Element Name Lanthanum (La) is a chemical element that belongs to the lanthanide series, a group of rare earth metals.
Atomic Mass 138.9055 u
Atomic Number 57
Number of Isotopes The most stable isotope is La-139, used in nuclear and scientific applications.
Mass Number (Isotopes) 137, 138, 139 (La-139 is the most abundant)
Melting Temperature 920 °C
Boiling Temperature 3460 °C
Density 6.15 g/cm³
Electronegativity 1.1 (Pauling Scale)
Year of Discovery 1839 (Carl Gustaf Mosander)
Abundance Found in minerals such as monazite and bastnäsite.
Characteristics
  • Soft, malleable silver-white metal.
  • Easily oxidizes in contact with air and water.
  • Has paramagnetic properties.
Technological Applications
  • Advanced optics: Used for special telescope and camera lenses.
  • Metal alloys: Improves mechanical properties of some metals.
  • Petrochemical catalysts: Enhances efficiency in oil refining.
  • Rechargeable NiMH batteries: Used in hybrid vehicles and electronics.
Deposit Locations
  • China: Largest producer of rare earth elements.
  • United States: Mainly in California and Alaska.
  • Australia: Found in monazite minerals.
  • Brazil: Some mineral reserves available.
Health Effects Not highly toxic, but may cause irritation if inhaled.
Environmental Effects Extraction can cause soil and water contamination.
Use in Phones
  • Screen: Improves color quality in glass screens.
  • Camera Lenses: Used in optical lenses for clearer images.
  • Glass Polishing: Combined with other rare earth elements for smooth surfaces.
