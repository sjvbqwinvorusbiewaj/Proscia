Gold (Au)

Property Value
Element Name Gold (Au) is a chemical element belonging to Group 11 of the periodic table. It is known for its monetary value and is highly sought after.
Atomic Mass 196.96657 u
Atomic Number 79
Group 11 (Periodic Table)
Period 6 (Periodic Table)
Melting Temperature 1064 °C
Boiling Temperature 2836 °C
Density 19.32 g/cm³
Electronegativity 2.54 (Pauling Scale)
Year of Discovery Prehistoric Era (One of the first metals discovered by humans)
Abundance Found throughout Earth's crust in low concentrations, in both primary and secondary deposits.
Characteristics
  • A precious metal known for its monetary value.
  • Formed in deep space through catastrophic events like neutron star collisions.
  • Possibly the first metal used by humans, around 6000 BC.
Technological Applications
  • Used in electronic components (chips, printed circuits, connectors).
  • Employed in medical devices (dental tools, orthopedic implants).
  • Gold nanoparticles used in drug delivery (e.g., cancer treatments).
Deposit Locations
  • Found in Earth's crust.
  • Major reserves are in South Africa, Australia, and Russia.
Health Effects Generally safe for humans and does not cause adverse reactions. Some studies suggest therapeutic effects.
Environmental Effects Gold mining is harmful, causing deforestation, biodiversity loss, fine dust pollution, and greenhouse gas emissions.
