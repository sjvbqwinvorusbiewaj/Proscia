Terbium (Tb)

Property Value
Element Name Terbium (Tb) is a chemical element that belongs to the lanthanide series, also known as rare earth elements.
Atomic Mass 158.9 u
Atomic Number 65
Number of Isotopes 36 known isotopes
Mass Number (Isotopes) 135-171 (Terbium-159 is the only stable isotope)
Melting Temperature 1356 °C
Boiling Temperature 3230 °C
Density 8.27 g/cm³
Electronegativity 1.2 (Pauling scale)
Year of Discovery 1843 (Carl Gustaf Mosander)
Abundance 1.2 ppm (parts per million)
Characteristics
  • Silver-colored, shiny, and soft metal.
  • Part of the lanthanide group.
  • Stable in air but can slowly oxidize, forming a protective oxide layer.
  • Reacts with acids to form salts.
  • Has unique magnetic properties used in advanced materials.
Technological Applications
  • Phosphors in color displays.
  • High-performance magnets.
  • Lasers and optical devices.
  • Nuclear reactors.
  • Catalytic converters and clean energy materials.
Deposit Locations
  • China: Inner Mongolia, Bayan Obo.
  • USA: Mountain Pass mine (California).
  • Australia: Mount Wield.
  • Sweden: recently discovered deposits.
  • Africa: South Africa and Madagascar.
Health Effects Slightly toxic if ingested, irritating to skin and eyes upon direct contact.
Environmental Effects Mining may cause radioactive and toxic contamination.
