Neodymium (Nd) Information

Property Value
Element Name Neodymium (Nd) is a chemical element belonging to the rare earth group or lanthanides. It is a silver-colored, shiny metal.
Atomic Mass 144.242 u
Atomic Number 60
Number of Isotopes Neodymium has seven natural isotopes with mass numbers (A) 142, 143, 144, 145, 146, 148, and 150. Among these, 142, 144, and 146 are stable.
Mass Numbers (Isotopes) 142, 143, 144, 145, 146, 148, and 150
Melting Temperature 1021 °C
Boiling Temperature 3074 °C
Density 7.007 g/cm³
Electronegativity 1.14
Year of Discovery 1885 (discovered along with Praseodymium)
Abundance The average abundance in the Earth's crust is approximately 38 ppm (parts per million).
Characteristics
  • Belongs to the rare earth group or lanthanides.
  • Silver-colored and shiny, but oxidizes quickly in air.
  • Reacts slowly with cold water and quickly with hot water.
  • Found in electronic devices such as color TVs and fluorescent lamps.
  • Used in alloys for lighter flints.
  • The second most abundant rare earth element, nearly as common as copper.
Technological Applications
  • Magnets: Neodymium magnets are essential in hard drives, speakers, headphones, and microphones.
Deposit Locations
  • Found in minerals such as monazite and bastnäsite.
  • The majority of global reserves are in China, followed by Vietnam and Brazil.
  • Significant deposits discovered in Sweden and Norway.
Health Effects Neodymium dust and fumes can cause respiratory problems and liver damage. Neodymium magnets may interfere with medical devices such as pacemakers.
Environmental Effects Neodymium mining and processing can lead to water and soil pollution, biodiversity loss, and air contamination. It may be toxic to aquatic organisms.
