Europium (Eu)

Property Value
Element Name Europium
Symbol Eu
Atomic Mass 151.96 u
Atomic Number 63
Number of Isotopes 21
Melting Temperature 822 °C
Boiling Temperature 1529 °C
Density 5.264 g/cm³
Electronegativity 1.2 (Pauling Scale)
Year of Discovery 1901 (by Demarcay during studies on Samarium)
Abundance One of the least abundant rare earth elements, similar to tin.
Characteristics
  • Soft, silvery rare and expensive metal.
  • Part of the lanthanide group.
  • Forms oxide layers when exposed to oxygen.
  • Most reactive element among lanthanides.
Technological Applications
  • Phosphors for color displays and LEDs.
  • Lasers and optical materials.
  • Control rods in nuclear reactors.
  • Fluorescent inks used in banknotes.
Major Deposits Location Primarily produced in China and the United States (~100 tons per year).
Health Effects Europium salts may be slightly toxic if ingested, though their toxicity has not been fully studied.
Environmental Effects Not harmful to plants or animals, but metallic dust may pose an explosion risk.
