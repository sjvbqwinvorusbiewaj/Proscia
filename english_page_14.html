Yttrium (Y)

Property Value
Element Name Yttrium
Atomic Mass 88.9059 g/mol
Atomic Number 39
Number of Isotopes 10
Isotope Mass (Most Abundant) 88.9059
Melting Temperature 1500 °C
Boiling Temperature 3336 °C
Density 4.47 g/cm³ at 20°C
Electronegativity 1.22
Year of Discovery 1794
Abundance 0.03% in Earth's crust
Characteristics A highly crystalline, iron-gray metal belonging to the rare earth group. It oxidizes quickly when heated.
Technological Applications
  • Used in red phosphors for color TV screens.
  • Strengthens magnesium and aluminum alloys.
  • Creates heat-resistant materials when alloyed with chromium and aluminum.
  • Used in impact-resistant glass for camera lenses.
  • Essential in superconductors.
Deposit Locations Found in minerals such as gadolinite, xenotime, and polycrase.
Health Effects Prolonged inhalation may lead to lung issues and accumulation in the liver.
Environmental Effects Accumulates in soil and water, harming aquatic organisms and increasing health risks.
