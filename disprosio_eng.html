Dysprosium (Dy)

Property Value
Element Name Dysprosium
Atomic Mass 162.5 u
Atomic Number 66
Number of Isotopes 33 known, with 7 stable isotopes (Dy-156, Dy-158, Dy-160, Dy-161, Dy-162, Dy-163, Dy-164).
Mass Number (Natural Isotopes) 156, 158, 160, 161, 162, 163, 164
Melting Temperature 1,412 °C
Boiling Temperature 2,567 °C
Density 8.54 g/cm³
Electronegativity 1.22 (Pauling Scale)
Year of Discovery 1886 (Paul Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran)
Abundance in Earth's Crust Approximately 6.2 mg/kg
Characteristics
  • Rare earth metal.
  • Bright silver color.
  • Oxidation-resistant at room temperature.
  • Paramagnetic at room temperature, ferromagnetic below -188 °C.
  • Reactive with water and acids.
Technological Applications
  • High-performance permanent magnets.
  • Lasers and optical components.
  • Nuclear reactor components (neutron absorber).
  • Electronic devices (hard drives, speakers).
  • Lamps and halogen vapor lanterns.
  • Additive in alloys for improved heat resistance.
Major Deposits Location
  • China (80% of global production).
  • Australia, India, Russia, Brazil, USA.
  • Extracted from minerals such as monazite and bastnäsite.
Health Effects
  • Non-toxic in low concentrations.
  • Compounds may irritate skin and mucous membranes.
  • Rare dust exposure can cause respiratory issues.
Environmental Effects
  • Low environmental risk when used properly.
  • Mining and refining may produce radioactive waste (due to thorium presence).
  • Possible contamination of soil and water if improperly disposed.
